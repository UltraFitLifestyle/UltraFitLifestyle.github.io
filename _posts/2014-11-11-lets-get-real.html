I am as much a fan of social media as the next person. I actually spend a lot of time focusing on social media marketing. However, social media is no substitute for real, human interaction. I think we sometimes forget that picking up the phone, or stopping by to see someone is more important than any “like” or comment.

Many people (including me) send out direct or indirect requests for help, prayer, or advice. But, how many of these virtual standers-by will actually DO something for you when you are truly in need. If someone posts that a loved one or pet has passed away, posting a comment saying “You are in my prayers” is wonderful. However, in loss, we human beings need other human beings for support...in the flesh, bearing covered dishes and hugs!

Even more close to home for me lately is the “moms helping moms” network. If a fellow mom is in need of an “emergency play date” in order to get something important accomplished, I am nearly always game. I will keep your kid, feed him or her, and be sure they are entertained. I will Like and Comment on your post begging for help. And, I will also follow up with a private message, text, or phone call to see if there is anything I can REALLY do to help you.

In business, and in friendship, the personal is the most valuable. Pick up the phone and get together with your friends. It’s kind of a lost art. You won’t regret it!!
