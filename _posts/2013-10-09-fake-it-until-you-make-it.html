I was at an event recently and a friend of mine said, “You know, your Facebook posts are just a little bit TOO positive sometimes….”

I had to laugh, and respond that I know I post a LOT of positive images, quotes, and links, but there is a reason. It is NOT because my life is so perfect. My Facebook posts are one part to motivate and inspire YOU, and one part to motivate and inspire ME!!!

My life is certainly not easy, with a job at Litchfield Dance Arts Academy, another job as a Realtor at Litchfield Real Estate, and a third job as an Independent Beachbody Coach, as well as a 5-year old and a husband….it’s a lot! I also live with Crohn’s disease, which is a challenge in itself. But, I am not complaining; I LOVE my life. However, it does not mean that I am not faced daily with things going wrong, things falling apart, and people getting angry at me. It’s ALL in the ATTITUDE!

As a society, we are pummeled daily, hourly, and sometimes on Twitter, up to the second with negative, upsetting, and downright awful news from our families, friends, and media. What to do?? Well, inspired by one of my favorite authors, Darren Hardy (author of The Compound Effect), I like to “flush my glass” daily.

The concept is that if you take a glass filled with dirty, icky, yucky water and flush it with enough CLEAN water, it will eventually be all clean, clear, and ready to drink. Same thing with your mind: if you are constantly faced with challenges, bad news, and stressful information, try to “flush your glass” so to speak, by listening to positive audio, reading positive motivational books, meditating…..or visiting my Facebbook page (haha!!). Even if you are NOT feeling happy or positive, you can “fake” your way there by filling your “glass” with the good stuff. Many forms of therapy will suggest you pretend you are happy until you really are!!!

I also believe that we ALL need motivation, whether it is to workout, eat better, or focus more on things we need to get done or improve upon. I know every time I post something positive and motivational, I COULD be helping just ONE person get through a tough moment. That’s all worth it to me.

I don’t want everyone to think I am Miss Mary Sunshine, because I am certainly NOT that. I am no angel. I have plenty of character flaws, issues, and hangups….not to mention that pesky chronic disease. But, I am on a daily uphill battle to BE better, DO better, and try to make someone else’s life a little sunnier, if only for a moment.

Best wishes of heath and happiness today!!!!
